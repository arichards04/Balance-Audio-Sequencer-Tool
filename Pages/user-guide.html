    Play and Stop Buttons

    The play and stop buttons control if audio is being played through the sequencers. To hear your composition back, press the play button. To stop the audio playing, press the stop button.

    Tempo Control

    This tempo controller operates as a text input. To change the tempo of the project, simply type in your desired bpm and the project will update in real time, even if audio is playing. Please note, this tempo control has a limited range of 60bpm to 200bpm. Therefore, the project tempo cannot go above or below this range.

    Master Volume

    The master volume slider adjusts the volume of all four channels at the same time. Moving the slider to 0 will mute the audio for all channels, until the slider is moved again. Similarly, moving the master volume slider to 10 will make the audio for each channel very loud. Please take care when adjusting to high volumes and only do so for a minimal amount of time as to not damage your hearing.

    Mixer Controls

    The mixing controls for each channel consist of instrument selection, volume control, pan control, effect selection and effect control. These options are consistent through the sequencer channels for ease of learning.

    Instrument Selection

    This option is demonstated in the form of a dropdown menu, labelled by "Select an instrument:". You may select from Guitar, Piano, Bass and Drums. If you would like to switch instruments on a channel, you must refresh your browser to do so. However, this will result in the loss of your current composition, so do so with caution.

    Volume

    Shown in the form of a blue slider, labelled "Channel Volume:", which controls the overall output volume for that particular channel. The range of this control is 0 (completely muted) to 10 (extremely loud). Please take care when adjusting to high volumes and only do so for a minimal amount of time as to not damage your hearing.

    Pan

    Shown in the form of a green slider, labelled "Pan Control" which controls the directional output for that particular channel. For example, you may wish to change the direction of the audio, to output from your left speaker. Do so by moving the slider to the left. The output label will then change from "C" to "L" to reflect this change. This control is limited to three directional options: "L" (hard left), "C" (central) and "R" (hard right).

    Effect Selection

    This option is demonstated in the form of two radio buttons, labelled "Reverb" and "Delay". These effects will only be able to be heard by moving the "Effect Control:" slider. You can of course choose not to have an effect on your channel, in which case, the Effect Control slider will remain bypassed. You may only choose one at effect per channel as a maximum.

    Effects Slider

    Shown in the form of a yellow slider, labelled "Effect Control" which controls two different things, depending on which effect is selected. If you have not selected an effect, this slider will remain bypassed and you will hear clean, unchanged audio. The range of this slider begins at 0 (minimum, completely clean audio) and ends at 1 (maximum, completely altered audio).

    Reverb

    If you have selected reverb, the Effect Control slider will control the "Room Size" of the reverb. Please take care when adjusting to the maximum output on "Reverb" as the output is loud. Only do so for a minimal amount of time as to not damage your hearing.

    Delay

    If you have selected delay, the Effect Control slider will control the "Delay Time". This meaning the amount of time between the original audio playing and the delayed audio following.

    Step-Sequencer

    The sequencers for each channel are where you will create your compositions. All sequencers are inactive when first loading the site to allow compositions to be created. Each sequencer is in the key of G major.

    By clicking on the indiviudal buttons in the sequencers, you will hear the corresponding note from the instrument you selected. These buttons will then become "active" and ready to be played back once the play button has been pressed.
